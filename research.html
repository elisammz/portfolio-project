I am a biomedical scientist by education. During my PhD, I investigated the origin and mechanism underlying the protein and cell resistance of anti-biofouling self-assembled monolayers (SAMs). I specialize in atomic force microscopy (AFM) and infrared spectroscopy for materials characterization.

I have experience conducting biomedical research for academic and industry in the fields of surface science, biosensing and radiolabeled markers. During my formal education, I have been continuously collecting, processing, modeling, and analyzing data to extract insights that support scientific findings.
